1. Saint Joseph Parish Church, commonly known as Dingras Church, is a Roman Catholic church located in the municipality of Dingras, Ilocos Norte, Philippines under the jurisdiction of the Diocese of Laoag.
Dingras started as a visita of Batac and became an independent parish by the Augustinians.

2. Madongan Dam is a breathtaking sight with its wide curtain-like cascading waters, formed by the convergence of Padsan River and Madongan River in Dingras, Ilocos Norte. The dam serves as a vital water source for nearby towns and has crystal-clear, shallow water teeming with small fish. Despite some issues with cleanliness, visitors can enjoy the fresh and cold water while relaxing in cottages or exploring nearby sari-sari stores.

3. Pikkan falls its a falls that the fresh water came from the mountains which you can drink, swim or anything you can do on the falls. Many tourists or foreign were visiting especially when summer seasons. This pikkan falls locates at Brgy. Capasan Dingras, Ilocos Norte.
